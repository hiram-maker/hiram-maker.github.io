Dr. Danforth Miller is a hypothesis-driven engineer, scientist, and inventor who brings broad experience in leveraging pharmaceutics, materials science, engineering, and computational modeling to drive effective development of drug products.

As a subject matter expert on multiple inhaled drug product approvals, Miller holds specific expertise in the formulation and physical characterization of small molecules and proteins to prevent and solve problems in applied research and product development environments. He has led multidisciplinary teams conducting early-phase preclinical and clinical development over 25 years at startups and large pharmaceutical companies, including Inhale Therapeutic Systems (Nektar Therapeutics), Novartis, Respira Therapeutics, and cystetic Medicines.

He is the author of 30+ scientific articles and nine issued patents, advancing particle engineering for pulmonary delivery of dry powder aerosols. He serves on the Editorial Advisory Board of the Journal of Pharmaceutical Sciences and holds a B.S. in Chemical Engineering (University of Colorado Boulder) and a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering (University of Wisconsin–Madison).

Dr. Jeffry Weers is an accomplished pharmaceutical professional and scientific innovator focused on pushing the frontiers of particle engineering for biomedical applications.

For 25 years, Weers has specialized in formulation and device design for respiratory drug delivery. A prolific inventor, he holds nearly 300 issued patents worldwide and contributed to seven approved products including TOBI® Podhaler™.

He has published more than 100 peer‑reviewed articles, is a well‑cited author and speaker, and has received several honors including the 2017 Alexander Fleming Serendipity Award. Weers serves on editorial boards including the Journal of Aerosol Medicine and Pulmonary Drug Delivery and is an Associate Editor for Frontiers in Drug Delivery.
